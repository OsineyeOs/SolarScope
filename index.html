Inverter

Inverter Required: Watts

Pure Sine Wave: Also known as a "true" sine wave, it powers sensitive electronics such as laptops, air conditioners, and refrigerators. Modified Sine Wave: Provides a stair-stepped approximation of a sine wave, ideal for devices like TVs, lights, and power tools. Square Wave: The simplest form of inverter output, converting DC to a basic AC signal, though it’s rarely used. Grid-Tied: The most common type, supplying AC power from a DC source while remaining synchronized with the grid. Off-Grid: Designed for standalone operation, it does not synchronize with the grid. Hybrid: Combines on-grid and off-grid capabilities by integrating solar, grid, and battery power, allowing energy storage and surplus export.

Inverter size is calculated by summing the total maximum load of all appliances and adding a 50% safety margin. This ensures the inverter can handle peak loads and provides a buffer for future expansion.

Battery

Battery Bank Voltage: affects the efficiency and cost of your system. Higher voltages reduce current, minimizing losses and allowing for thinner cables, but require more batteries in series.

Battery Bank:

Number of Batteries:

Battery bank size is determined by the total daily energy consumption of the appliances, the battery voltage, and the depth of discharge. A higher battery capacity ensures longer backup time.

Solar Panel

Solar Panels Required:

The number of solar panels is calculated based on the battery bank size, the panel wattage, and the daily sunlight hours. More panels ensure faster battery charging.

Charge Controller

Charge Controller: Amps

PWM: Less expensive, good for smaller systems. MPPT: More efficient, maximizes power from solar panels.

Charge controller size is determined by the voltage of the solar array, the voltage of the battery bank, and the amperage. It regulates the voltage and current from the solar panels to the batteries, preventing overcharging.
